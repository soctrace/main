# Mijas Territorial Context

This document is an internal, RAG-friendly territorial summary. It does not
replace observed data. Characteristics and model implications are contextual
hypotheses unless independently validated by approved datasets.

## Mijas Pueblo

Sections: `2907001001`, `2907001002`.

Context: historical mountain nucleus, strong local identity, community
cohesion, traditions and potentially dense informal information networks.

Model implications: test for stronger localist sensitivity and candidate
familiarity effects. Do not apply them deterministically.

Confidence: medium-high.

## Las Lagunas

Historical core: `2907001003`, `2907001004`, `2907001005`, `2907001006`.

Dense expansion: `2907001020`, `2907001025`, `2907001037`.

Mixed expansion: `2907001033`.

Context: urban and dense settlement pattern, historical working-class roots,
growth linked to construction and proximity to Fuengirola, decentralized daily
life and potentially greater service pressure.

Model implications: test for a PSOE local floor in older areas and higher
abstention sensitivity in dense or transitional areas. Keep origin and
expansion areas separate.

Confidence: medium. Section `2907001033` is medium-low due to mixed definition.

## La Cala And Coastal-Touristic Corridor

Core: `2907001007`.

Coastal and tourism-residential expansion: `2907001011`, `2907001023`,
`2907001027`, `2907001030`.

Context: former fishing settlement and coastal tourism-residential corridor,
with international residential presence and higher-value expansion pockets.

Model implications: test internationalization and residential-mobility effects.
Interpret turnout carefully because total population and voting census are not
equivalent.

Confidence: medium.

## Rural And Dispersed Settlements

Sections: `2907001013`, `2907001017`, `2907001032`, `2907001035`.

Related mixed villa expansion: `2907001036`.

Context: dispersed housing, rural legacy and heterogeneous coexistence of older
settlements with tourism-residential pockets.

Model implications: moderate forecast confidence where averages may conceal
internal heterogeneity. Test sensitivity to infrastructure, land regulation and
municipal services without assuming a fixed political direction.

Confidence: medium-low to medium depending on section.
