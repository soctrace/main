# Mijas Political Context

## Verifiable Electoral Facts

These facts should be cross-checked against approved electoral marts before use.

- During the 1980s and 1990s, PSOE held the mayoralty for an extended period.
- In 2007, PSOE remained the leading municipal force while PP became a strong alternative.
- In 2011, PP won an absolute majority and localist actors obtained representation.
- In 2015, municipal fragmentation increased and Cs entered the council strongly.
- In 2019, PSOE and Cs supported the municipal government context; PP did not recover its 2011 result.
- In 2023, PSOE obtained 10 councillors, PP 9, VOX 3, Cs 2 and Por Mi Pueblo 1.
- After the 2023 election, a motion of censure supported by PP, VOX and Por Mi Pueblo resulted in Ana Mata becoming mayor.

## Interpretive Hypotheses

### PSOE Local Floor

PSOE may retain a local floor linked to historical embeddedness, social
networks, older Las Lagunas sections and a known local candidate.

Model use: test municipal PSOE stability and avoid importing national trends
mechanically. Confidence: medium.

### PP Brand Reserve

PP may have a structural reserve where regional or national PP performance is
stronger than its municipal performance.

Model use: calculate the observed cross-election gap by section. Confidence:
medium-high when supported by data.

### PP Candidate Reset Potential

The leadership change may reduce previous local candidate drag.

Model use: expose a cautious contextual potential derived from observed PP
brand reserve. Do not treat it as a direct vote transfer. Confidence: medium.

### Conservative Localist Split Risk

A future conservative localist offer could fragment PP recovery potential.

Model use: uncertainty only until electoral supply is human-confirmed. The V1
adjustment keeps this hypothesis inactive. Confidence: medium-low.

### Cs Orphan Vote Pool

Declining Cs support should be treated as an uncertain transfer pool. It may
flow toward several parties or abstention.

Model use: expose pool size and uncertainty; do not distribute mechanically.
Confidence: medium.

### Por Mi Pueblo Localist Transfer Pool

Por Mi Pueblo support may have personalist or localist components.

Model use: expose an uncertain localist pool without deterministic transfer.
Confidence: medium-low to medium.

### VOX National Anchor

VOX municipal support may be partly anchored to regional or national dynamics.

Model use: compare municipal, regional and national signals; avoid purely local
causal claims. Confidence: medium.

### Territorial Cluster Effect

Mijas should not be modeled as a homogeneous unit.

Model use: add cluster-level contextual sensitivity and uncertainty, never a
deterministic party label. Confidence: medium-high.
